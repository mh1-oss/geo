Procedures (According to ASTM D1143)1.Slow Maintained Load (SM)2.Quick Maintained Load (QM)3.Constant Rate of Penetration (CRP)4.Swedish Cyclic Test (SC)
Test Procedures (ASTM D1143-81)Slow Maintained Load Test (SM)–Most accurate and widely used–Duration: 40–70 hours–Load applied in 8 increments 0%,25, 50, 75, 100, 125, 150, 175% up to 200% of design load–For each load increment: record settlement for 1 hour. If the rate is ≤0.25 mm/hr, move to the next load. If it’s >0.25 mm/hr, maintain the load for up to 2 hours.–200% load maintained for 24 hours or 12 hr if the settlement recorded less than 0.25 mm/hr.
Test Procedures (ASTM D1143-81)Slow Maintained Load Test (SM)–Unload the pile in four steps, each step removing 25% of the applied load, each unloading step (1 hr). –After unloading (load became zero), –If working pile left the pile without loading for 12 hr and record the restrained for pile.–For a trial (failure) pile: reload in 4 steps up to 200% of the design load, with 20 minutes between steps. After reaching 200%, continue loading in 10% increments up to 300%, keeping 20 minutes between each increment. Or Continued until pile failure
Test Procedures (ASTM D1143-81)Slow Maintained Load Test (SM)–Unload the pile in four steps, each step removing 25% of the applied load, each unloading step (1 hr) and pile left without loading for 12 hr and record the restrained for pile.–The main disadvantage is that the test takes a long time, often 40–70  (48) hours or more.

Test Procedures (ASTM D1143-81)2-Quick Maintained Load Test (QM)–Continue adding load increments until the test load is reached (300% of design load) .–After a 5-minute interval, –remove the full load in four equal decrements, with 5 minutes between each decrement.–After unloading left the pile 15 minute and record the settlement
Test Procedures (ASTM D1143-81)2-Quick Maintained Load Test (QM)–Advantages:–Fast (3–5 hrs) and economical–Dissadvantages: –Not suitable for settlement prediction.

Constant Rate of Penetration (CRP)–Constant pile movement rate of 0.05 in/min.–Force required to maintain rate is recorded.–Pros: Fast. Cons: High load needed for end-bearing piles.
Swedish Cyclic Test (SC)–Repeated loading/unloading cycles.–Used mainly in research or cyclic loading structures.–Cons: Time-consuming; modifies pile behavior.

Chin Hyperbolic MethodChin’s method identifies the ultimate load using a hyperbolic relationship between load and settlement.Plot Δ/Qₐ versus Δ,where Δ = movement and Qₐ = applied load.The resulting plot should form a straight line (hyperbolic trend).The inverse of the slope of this line gives the ultimate pile load.

6-Mazurkiewicz’s Method–Plot the load–movement curve.–Select equal pile-head movements → draw vertical linesto intersect the curve, then draw horizontal linesto the load axis.–From each load intersection, draw a 45° lineto intersect the next load line.
6-Mazurkiewicz’s Method–These intersection points form an approximate straight line.–Extend this straight line to the vertical load axis→ the intersection gives the failure load.–Assumes the curve is approximately parabolic.–Failure load is typically close to the 80% criterion, but some judgment is needed because points may not align perfectly.

7-Fuller and Hoy’s Method–Plot the load–movement curve.–Identify the failure load where the tangent slopeof the curve equals 0.05 in/ton.–Method applies to QM tests.–Main disadvantage: it penalizes long piles, since their larger elastic movements reach the 0.05 in/ton slope earlier.
8-Butler and Hoy’s Method–Plot the load–movement curve.–The failure load is the intersection of the 0.05 in/ton slope linewith either:–the initial straight portionof the curve, or–a line parallel to the rebound (elastic) linefrom the origin.–Applicable to QM tests.
